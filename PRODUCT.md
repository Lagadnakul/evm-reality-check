# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Skeptical Indian citizens who have encountered EVM tampering narratives on social media or in political discourse. They arrive doubtful and need to leave feeling genuinely informed — not lectured. Secondary: students and researchers studying Indian democracy or election technology who need depth and credibility to cite.

## Product Purpose

EVM Reality Check is a public-interest educational site that confronts the most common myths about India's Electronic Voting Machines with technical fact. Success looks like a skeptic finishing the page with their doubt replaced by understanding — and being able to explain the security model to someone else.

## Brand Personality

Modern · Bold · Transparent

Voice: confident and direct, never defensive. Tone: a sharp journalist who has done the research — not a government spokesman, not a conspiracy debunker. Presents evidence and lets it speak.

## Anti-references

- **eci.gov.in** — the stiff, bureaucratic, table-heavy official government portal aesthetic. Avoid anything that reads as institutional, form-heavy, or dated.
- **Generic SaaS landing page** — cream/sand background, metric cards with big numbers, soft blue CTAs, Inter-only typography, gradient orb decorations, glassmorphism cards, uppercase eyebrow kickers above every heading.
- **Cyberpunk / hacker aesthetic** — neon glows, terminal-green on black, matrix-style rain effects. Does not build civic trust.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence first.** Every section resolves a specific doubt with a specific fact. Design hierarchy should put the claim and its rebuttal at the same visual weight — not bury the fact under decoration.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** A secure system doesn't need to shout. Use one accent color, precise typography, and deliberate whitespace. Ornamentation is the enemy of credibility.
3. **The Atlantic at midnight.** Editorial dark: high contrast, a serif display face for authority, a sans for clarity. The page should feel like serious long-form journalism, not a product launch.
4. **Transparency is structural.** The security architecture is the content — don't visualize it with identical icon cards. Let the prose carry the weight; use layout to reveal complexity, not flatten it.
5. **One truth marker.** The verified-green accent appears only where something is confirmed true — fact pills, checkmarks, "Reality" labels. Overuse dilutes it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum; target AAA for body text (≥7:1 contrast)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animations (crossfade or instant)
- Semantic HTML throughout — single-page with landmark regions
- No information conveyed by color alone; pair with shape or text
